# Strategy DNA: Alex Hormozi (Acquisition.com / Value-First)

> **Core Focus:** Scaling businesses through high-trust, value-first content.
> **Mindset:** "Give until they feel like a jerk for not buying from you."
> **Niche:** Business Scaling, Offers, Sales, M&A, Personal Development.

---

## 1. High-Leverage Logic (The Framework)

Hormozi's strategy revolves around **Asymmetric Leverage**:

1.  **Complexity of Delegation:** Beginner AI use is "one in, one out." Elite use is delegating **10-25 step processes**. ROI is tied to delegation depth.
2.  **Price Anchoring:** Never sell the "thing" (AI/Ads); sell the **Outcome** (Customers). Anchor your $50k price against a $500k traditional alternative.
3.  **The Foundation Rule:** Speed of growth means nothing if the "bricks" are flimsy. Sometimes you must restart at zero to build for a $10M+ outcome.
4.  **The 1,000 Rule:** Mastery is a volume game. Perform "unreasonable" repetitions until it's statistically impossible to still be bad.

---

## 2. Strategic Hook Formulas (The Truth Bomb)

Hormozi hooks focus on **Directness, Transparency, and Absurdity**:

- **The "Truth Bomb" Hook:** "I spent $1M on ads and got $0 in return. Here's exactly why I failed."
- **The "Asymmetric Outcome" Hook:** "How can we charge $50k for AI ads when most charge $500? Because clients don't buy AI; they buy customers."
- **The "Reality Check" Hook:** "Skill in AI is proportional to the complexity of the tasks you can delegate."
- **The "Volume" Hook:** "Do so much you can't suck. Here's how to hit 1,000 repetitions in half the time."

---

## 3. Retention Tactics (Radical Transparency)

To keep viewers, Hormozi uses **Proof-Stacking and Raw Storytelling**:

- **Social Proof Stacking:** "We've scaled 40+ companies to $100M+... here's the pattern I see."
- **The "Underdog" Pivot:** Talking about the time he slept on a gym floor or his parents' house to make it work.
- **Micro-Dose of Math:** Using quick, understandable numbers to prove a point (e.g., "If you have 100 leads and close 10... that's 10%").

---

## 4. The Conversion Strategy (The "Value-Trap")

Hormozi's CTA is always about **Lowering the Barrier to Entry**.

- **Primary CTA:** "If you want the full training, I have a free book/course. No email required."
- **Secondary CTA:** "I don't have anything to sell you. Just apply this to your business."

---

## 5. Enrichment Rules (How to "Upgrade" a Creator)

When using Alex Hormozi's strategy to enrich a script:

- **Step 1: The "Outcome" Pivot.** Ensure the script focuses on the *result* (customers/money) rather than the *process* (AI/tools).
- **Step 2: Apply the "Price Anchor."** Contrast the current cost/effort against a much more expensive traditional alternative to create high perceived value.
- **Step 3: High-Leverage Framing.** Position the advice as a "system" or "agent" that handles multiple steps, rather than a single one-off tip.
- **Step 4: Remove the "Salesy" tone.** Make the volume and value so high that the viewer feels dumb for not sticking around. Focus on the "How-To" over the "What-If."
