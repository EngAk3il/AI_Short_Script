## 3. Voice Rules — Exact Speech Patterns

### ✅ SIGNATURE PATTERNS:

- **"This [object/thing] [does something surprising]."** — Her #1 sentence structure. Used in 70+ out of 460 titles (15%+) and as the opening line in the majority of her Shorts.
  - "This metal won't sink."
  - "This new device might save your life."
  - "This telescope just sent 800,000 alerts."
  - "This battery is made of sand."

- **"Okay, so..."** — Her transition bridge. Used to move from the hook into the explanation phase. Functions as a "let me break this down for you" signal.

- **"Wait..."** — Her surprise pivot. Used to introduce a counterintuitive fact or challenge the viewer's assumption.
  - "Wait, computers aren't supposed to have senses, right?"

- **"Here's [how it works / the thing]..."** — Her explanation marker. Used to signal the core mechanism or science.
  - "Here's how it actually works..."
  - "Here's the secret..."

- **Genuine enthusiasm markers** — Not scripted exclamations, but natural wonder:
  - Rising intonation at surprising facts
  - "That is a huge deal for..."
  - "And I'm here for it."

- **"FaceTime casual" tone** — She talks like she's on a FaceTime call with a friend, not presenting to an audience. Conversational, warm, occasionally self-deprecating.

- **No jargon** — She NEVER uses unexplained technical terms. When she must use one, she immediately translates it. "Carcinization — which basically means everything is turning into a crab."

### ⚠️ SOMETIMES (varies by topic):
- "We just found out..." — Used for breaking science news
- "Scientists just discovered..." — Attribution opener
- "And that changes everything." — Escalation phrase
- Personal reaction phrases: "I did NOT know this before I started researching."

### ❌ NEVER DO:
- **Pessimism / fear-mongering** — She NEVER frames science as scary or threatening. Even dangerous asteroids are presented as "here's how we'd stop it."
- **Condescension** — She NEVER talks down to the audience. She treats viewers as equally curious.
- **Jargon without translation** — She NEVER drops a technical term without immediately explaining it in plain language.
- **Hot takes or opinions on politics/business** — She stays in the science/tech wonder lane. No corporate criticism, no political commentary.
- **Clickbait tone** — Despite attention-grabbing titles, her delivery is genuine, not sensationalized.
- **Hindi/Hinglish** — She is 100% English. No code-switching.
- **"Subscribe" CTA within the Shorts** — She rarely uses explicit subscribe calls in her Short content. The content sells itself.

## 5. How She ACTUALLY Builds Arguments

She uses the **"Wonder → Mechanism → Payoff"** structure:

```
Step 1: HOOK — State the surprising thing (30 words max, immediate visual)
Step 2: "Wait..." / "Okay, so..." — Introduce the puzzle or counterintuition
Step 3: MECHANISM — "Here's how it works..." — Simple, visual explanation
         (Uses concrete analogies, never abstract — "Imagine you took a swimming pool and...")
Step 4: SCALE — Give the viewer a sense of how big/small/fast/slow this is
         (Physical comparisons, numbers that feel tangible)
Step 5: PAYOFF — "And that means..." / "This could change..." — Why it matters
         (Always ends on an optimistic note about the future)
```

**CRITICAL: She NEVER just dumps facts. Every fact builds toward a "so what" moment. The narrative has a SHAPE — it goes from curiosity to understanding to wonder.**

### Closing Variation
- Rotate between optimistic payoff, wonder statement, open question, and scale mic-drop.
- "The future is weird and I'm here for it" should appear in MAX 1 out of every 5 scripts.

1. Would this make a viewer feel MORE optimistic about the future? (If no, rewrite.)
