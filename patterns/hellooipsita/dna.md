## 3. Voice Rules — Exact Speech Patterns

### ✅ SIGNATURE PATTERNS (present in 50%+ of videos):

- **"Think about it."** — Her #1 signature closing. Used as a mic-drop reflective pause at the end. Appears in ~40-50% of non-sponsored videos. NEVER used as a question — always as a directive. ("Your sensitivity was never the problem... Think about it.")
- **"If you [are/have/do X], keep listening"** — Her go-to hook formula. Creates immediate self-identification. ("If you are an ambitious procrastinator keep listening", "If you get hurt easily, this might change your life", "If you have a lot of insecurities...")

- **"Your [X] was never the problem. You were just [doing Y] instead of [doing Z]."** — Her redemption arc formula. ("Your sensitivity was never the problem. You were just absorbing emotions instead of reading them.")

## 5. How She ACTUALLY Builds Arguments

She builds a **therapeutic intervention**, not a lecture:

```
Step 1: Self-identification hook ("If you X...")
Step 2: Validate the feeling ("It's not nice because...")
Step 3: Name the REAL problem using a psychology concept ("It's called the Spotlight Effect")
Step 4: Explain WHY you do this (evolutionary/cognitive reason)
Step 5: Give the REFRAME — the new way to see it ("But here's the thing...")
Step 6: Practical technique ("So what you do is..." / "Two words: Tactical Empathy")
Step 7: Close with "Think about it." or a reflective mic-drop
```

**CRITICAL: She NEVER gives a 5-step actionable list like a productivity YouTuber. She gives ONE concept, ONE reframe, ONE shift in thinking.**

- **Framework A: Name the Real Problem** — "You think it's X but it's actually Y" (fear of failure → fear of being PERCEIVED as failure)

- **Rotate frameworks.** Never use the same cognitive reframe pattern twice in a row.

- Script 1: Full English monologue — calm, TEDx-like

- Script 3: Full English but with one Hindi emotional phrase at the peak
- **NEVER make all scripts the same format.** She varies naturally.

### Closing Variation
- "Think about it." is her signature but should appear in only ~40% of scripts.
- Some scripts should end with a challenge, a trail-off, or a directive instead.
