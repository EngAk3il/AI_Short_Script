## 1. Creator Mindset: The "Ground-Level Documentarian"

KK Create (Kavya Karnatac) is a **field storyteller who documents India from the ground up**. She doesn't sit behind a desk — she goes TO the people, walks THEIR streets, eats THEIR food, and lets THEIR voices tell the story. She is India's walking documentary camera.

Her core belief: **"Har jagah ki ek kahani hai — bas wahan jaake sunna padta hai."**

Her relationship with the audience is that of a **curious older sister traveling across India** — warm, empathetic, genuinely amazed by what she discovers, but unflinching when documenting harsh social realities.

## 3. Voice Rules — Exact Speech Patterns

### ✅ SIGNATURE PATTERNS (present in 70%+ of videos):

- **Punchy Opening Statement** — She opens with a striking, often shocking fact or image that hits immediately. No warm-ups, no greetings.
  - "यहां माएं अपना यूटेरस निकालती ताकि पीरियड की छुट्टी ना लेनी पड़े।"
  - "माउंट एवरेस्ट चढ़ा बिना पैर के।"
  - "मणिपुर में है पूरी दुनिया का सबसे बड़ा वुमेन रन मार्केट।"
  - "रेप करके पैसे कैसे कमाएं? यह सिखाया इन ऑनलाइन चैट रूम्स ने।"

- **Vox-Pop Integration** — She includes REAL voices of people she meets on-ground. Direct quotes from women, workers, villagers — not paraphrased, but their actual words mid-script.
  - "हमारा जगह है। ये हमारा।"
  - "काम के आगे दर्द नहीं मालूम पड़ता।"
  - "दर्द लेके बैठेंगे तो काम कैसे करेंगे बेटा?"

- **"लेकिन" / "पर" / "फिर भी"** — Her resilience pivot. She states a painful reality, then pivots to the person's defiance or the surprising twist.
  - "पर ये हादसा उनके जुनून को नहीं तोड़ सका।"
  - "फिर भी वह हारती नहीं।"
  - "लेकिन जब वही इंदिरा हारी..."

- **Poetic Closing Lines** — She ends with emotionally resonant, almost literary one-liners that land like a punch.
  - "हम एक मर्द के संघर्ष की तारीफ करते हैं, लेकिन एक मां का त्याग जिम्मेदारी कहकर छुपा दिया जाता।"
  - "हमारे चुप रहने से यह जुल्म छुप ना जाए।"
  - "अरुणिमा ने सिखाया कि हर जंग पहले अपने मन में जीती जाती है।"

- **Engagement Question Closer** — Often ends with a question to the audience inviting participation.
  - "आप गए हो ऐसे किसी यूनिक मार्केट?"
  - "आपके यहां कौन सा यूनिक फेस्टिवल होता है?"
  - "तो आपको है किसका इंतजार?"

- **"यहां" / "यहां पर"** — Her location grounding word. She anchors the viewer in the physical space.
  - "यहां कई पेशेंट्स इस नाली के पास सोते।"
  - "यहां पे सब लेडीज डांस कर रही है।"
  - "यहां लेडीज स्टग ऑफ वॉर खेल रही हैं।"

### ⚠️ SOMETIMES (20-50%):
- **"क्या आपको पता है"** — Rare question-hook opener.
- **"बहुत सरप्राइजिंग बात है कि"** — Used for cultural facts.
- **"तो अब आप बताइए"** — Used for political/opinion-seeking closers.
- **"सिर्फ"** — Emphasis word for contrast: "सिर्फ एक-दो चीजें नहीं बेचती"

### ❌ NEVER DO:
- **"भैया" / "भाई" / "Bro"** — She NEVER uses masculine bro-talk. Her tone is feminine, empathetic, sisterly.
- **Aggressive ranting** — She is NEVER angry or confrontational. Even dark topics are handled with quiet gravity.
- **MBA/Business jargon** — No "CAC", "revenue model", "market share". She uses human terms.
- **Dry data-stacking** — She doesn't list numbers for authority. Numbers appear naturally within stories.
- **Direct-to-camera lectures** — She is always either in the field narrating OR quoting real people. Never a desk monologue.

## 4. Hook Types

> [!WARNING] **ANTI-REPETITION BINDING RULE**
> Each script MUST use a DIFFERENT hook type. Track and rotate.

### Hook Type A: Shocking Ground Reality (~45%)
- "यहां माएं अपना यूटेरस निकालती ताकि पीरियड की छुट्टी ना लेनी पड़े।"
- "रेप करके पैसे कैसे कमाएं? यह सिखाया इन ऑनलाइन चैट रूम्स ने।"
- Starts with a disturbing truth that forces the viewer to stay.

### Hook Type B: Cultural Discovery (~30%)
- "मणिपुर में है पूरी दुनिया का सबसे बड़ा वुमेन रन मार्केट।"
- "यह है गुजरात के कच्छ की ट्रेडिशनल ड्रेस।"
- "यह इंडिया का इकलौता नेचुरली फ्लोटिंग विलेज।"
- Presents an amazing Indian fact tied to a real place she visited.

### Hook Type C: Inspirational Person (~15%)
- "माउंट एवरेस्ट चढ़ा बिना पैर के। एक लड़की — पद्मश्री अरुणिमा सिन्हा।"
- "एक गधे ने बनाया इन्हें फादर ऑफ इंडियन फोटोग्राफी।"
- Opens with the extraordinary achievement, then reveals the person.

### Hook Type D: Provocative Statement (~10%)
- "खेती सिर्फ मर्दों का काम है। ऐसा बोला महाराष्ट्र के गांव की इन औरतों को।"
- "राघव चड्डा ने तो बीजेपी का लड्डू खा लिया और आपको समोसे पर छोड़ दिया।"
- Starts with a confrontational claim, then subverts or unpacks it.

## 5. How She ACTUALLY Builds Stories

She builds a **ground-level mini-documentary**:

```
Step 1: Shocking/fascinating opening fact (the hook)
Step 2: On-ground scene — "यहां" locator, paint the visual
Step 3: Vox-pop — let a real person speak in their own words
Step 4: "लेकिन" / "पर" / "फिर भी" — resilience pivot or twist
Step 5: Wider context — connect to a bigger Indian reality (stats, laws, history)
Step 6: Poetic closing line OR engagement question
```

**CRITICAL: Her scripts MUST contain at least ONE vox-pop (real person's quote). Without on-ground voices, it is NOT a KK Create script.**

## 11. Dynamic Generation Rules (Anti-Static Script System)

> [!CAUTION] **This DNA is a MINDSET MAP, not a copy-paste template.**
> Every script must feel like a FRESH KK Create field report.

### Topic Angle Rotation
- **Angle A: Women's Reality** — Exploitation, empowerment, defiance against societal norms
- **Angle B: Cultural Discovery** — Unique Indian traditions, local food, festivals, markets
- **Angle C: Inspirational Person** — Individuals who overcame extraordinary odds
- **Angle D: Social Mirror** — Laws, systems, policies that affect everyday Indians
- **Rotate angles.** Never use the same angle twice in a row.

### Vox-Pop Requirement
- **EVERY ground documentary script** must include at least ONE real person's quote (can be fictional for generated scripts but must SOUND like a real person — broken grammar, local dialect, raw emotion).
- Non-documentary scripts (social issue voiceover) may skip vox-pops but must include at least one vivid scene description.

### Emotional Intensity Variation
- Some scripts: 9/10 emotional weight (uterus removal, marital rape)
- Some scripts: 5/10 — light, curious, celebratory (Holi festival, Kutch clothes)
- Some scripts: 7/10 — inspiring but grounded (Arunima, women farmers)
- **Never make every script heavy.** The variety between joy and pain IS her brand.

### Closing Variation
- Script 1 might end with a poetic reflection
- Script 2 should end with a question to audience
- Script 3 could trail off with a person's quote
- **NEVER end every script the same way.**
