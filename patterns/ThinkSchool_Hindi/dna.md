## 3. Voice Rules — Exact Speech Patterns

### ✅ SIGNATURE PATTERNS:

- **Interview/Discussion Format** — Most Shorts are clipped from longer interviews. Two voices alternate rapidly.
  - Voice 1 asks, Voice 2 answers (or vice versa)
  - Quick cuts between perspectives

- **Hindi-English Business Mix** — Heavy code-switching between Hindi and English business terminology:
  - "अगर मैं कोई भी फीमेल हीरोइन लेने को गया उनका पर डे रेट वाज़ एनीवेयर बिटवीन 50 लैक्स टू अ क्रोर"
  - "ही हैज़ ज़ीरो ऑपोरर्चुनिटी कॉस्ट"

- **"करेक्ट।"** — Agreement filler used frequently to validate points.
- **"मोर इम्पोर्टेंटली"** — Escalation phrase before the key insight.
- **Specific ₹ amounts with business context** — "30-40 लाख का मार्केटिंग बजट", "3 डे कॉन्ट्रैक्ट", "25 करोड़ → 120 करोड़"

### ⚠️ SOMETIMES:
- "सो" (So) — Transition word (~30%)
- "बट" (But) — Pivot word (~25%)
- "OOH!" — Surprise reaction in interview format

### ❌ NEVER DO:
- **Solo monologue** — Almost all Shorts are interview/discussion format, NOT solo narration.
- **Pure Hindi without English business terms** — He ALWAYS uses English for business concepts.
- **Emotional/motivational content** — He is analytical, not inspirational.
- **Standard YouTube CTAs** — Shorts from interview clips rarely have explicit CTAs.

## 4. Hook Types

- "नवी मुंबई इंटरनेशनल एयरपोर्ट एक ऐसा प्रोजेक्ट है..."
- Project/opportunity introduction

## 5. Argument Structure (Interview Clip)

```
Step 1: Provocative opening line (problem or bold claim)
Step 2: "व्हाई सर?" / "करेक्ट" — dialogue back-and-forth
Step 3: Specific numbers and business logic
Step 4: "आईडिया आया" — the creative/strategic solution
Step 5: Result or punchline ("एंड इट वेंट वायरल")
```

## 11. Dynamic Generation Rules (Anti-Static Script System)

> [!CAUTION] **This DNA is a MINDSET MAP, not a copy-paste template.**

### Format Variation
- Most Shorts are INTERVIEW CLIPS (~75%). But sometimes generate a solo analysis (~25%).
- When generating interview clips, vary the dynamic: sometimes Voice 1 leads, sometimes Voice 2.

### Business Case Rotation
- **Case A: Marketing Genius** — How a brand used a creative hack (Siddharth Malhotra lipstick)
- **Case B: Consumer Behavior Insight** — Indian vs Korean product preferences
- **Case C: Investment/Real Estate** — Land, airports, opportunity analysis
- **Rotate.** Don't generate 3 marketing cases in a row.

### Punchline Variation
- Some clips end on a viral result ("And it went viral")
- Some end on a controversial hot take
- Some end mid-conversation, leaving tension
